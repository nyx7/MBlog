About me

I’m Mohamed, a 16-year-old student from Morocco in my final year of high school (2BAC Sciences Math). This is supposed to be the toughest year of my life so far — and I’m ready for it. My goal? To become one of the greats, someone remembered, someone whom others rely on and trust, someone worthy.

I’ve spent this past year learning — not just from textbooks, but from life. I learned Python, built my first apps, and now I’m exploring web development. I’ve been improving my English and French, preparing for competitive universities, and building better habits — discipline, focus, prayer, health. Some days I’m consistent; some days I fail. But I always come back stronger.

I’m not rich, I’m not a genius, but most importantly, I’m not waiting for a miracle. What I have is effort. I believe that hard work, faith, and curiosity can open doors no one expects you to walk through. I like to break records, solve problems, discover the unknown.

I don’t know exactly where this path will lead me — maybe the Top, maybe the Bottom, maybe somewhere completely unexpected — but I know I’m going to push with everything I’ve got. Because there’s only one way forward: earn it.
